Autonomous Map Making Mobile Robot

This research focuses on designing and assembling of an autonomous map making robot. This robot has the ability to create a map for a specific environment by the sensors that are integrated in the robot. Furthermore, the sensors provide the robot with object avoidance features, cognitive features and navigational control. We are using ultrasonic sensors for object avoidance and detecting the proximity of surrounding objects. We are also using inertial measurement unit (IMU) sensor to sense the acceleration and direction of the robot and to estimate the distance traveled by the robot. In addition, we are using a 3D high-definition camera with depth sensor to capture images and videos of the surrounding environment. We process images and video to recognize places that have been visited and to associate them with the created map. We are currently using this robot to scout through academic buildings at KFUPM and to create navigational maps augmented with real images.

  • Team: Mohannad - Omar
  • Date: March 2014 - March 2015
  • Type: Embedded System - Artifical Intelligence

RoboCup SSL

The RoboCup Small-Size robot soccer League (SSL) is a research domain to study multi-robot planning and execution under the uncertainty of real-time perception and motion in an adversarial environment. The RoboCup SSL is set as a soccer game between two teams of six robots each, playing with an orange golf ball in a field of predefined size with line markings.

I am the team leader of Embedded System Area in Robocup Team at KFUPM. We are aiming to participate in the competition Summer 2016.
